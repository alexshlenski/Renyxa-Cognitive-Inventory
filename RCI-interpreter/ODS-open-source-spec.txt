ðŸ”¸XREFs

In the square brackets you can see what is called Cross-reference Index or XREF
XREF is comprised of the Document ID ("NKREA" in this example) and a HEX index 
separated by a pipe character ( | )

XREF is used for cross-reference between the elements of the registry and the 
source text. The HEX part of XREF is incremented by 1 at each line monotonically 
across the entire file.

XREFs are used to create original document proof provenance of every detail across the Registry. 
Using the XREFs the field analysts can maintain cross-reference between KR layers that represent 
the semantics of the original document and the exact lines of the normalized document, from which
the facts are derived.

What an Aspect is.
An Aspect is a dependent feature that inheres in a bearer (an entity or an occurrent). 
Aspects let you capture what a thing is like (properties), comes from (lineage), can do (capacities), 
is for (functions), is designated as (roles/offices/memberships), is identified by (codes), and how 
it relates to other entities (relations, dependencies, standing causal influence).
Aspects are not timelines (thatâ€™s Occurrents) and not statuses/conditions (thatâ€™s State).

1) IntrinsicAttribute
Definition. Current, bearer-dependent properties observable/measurable now without needing a trigger 
or permission.

Mass, density, dimensions (length/width/height/diameter), shape; material (composition/grade); 
mechanical (hardness/elasticity); thermal (operating range); electrical (resistivity/ratings); 
optical (color/reflectance); acoustic signature; morphology/finish (markings/livery, camouflage, insignia). 
For persons/biological: blood type; biometrics (fingerprint/face/iris templates).

2) HistoricalAttribute

Definition. Origin/lineage facts fixed by manufacture or biography; they travel with the exemplar across time.

Design lineage: Make, Model
Manufacture & origin: Factory/Plant, CountryOfOrigin, ProductionDate
Provenance (summary): FirstOwner/Registrant, AcquisitionMethod
Biographic (person): DateOfBirth, PlaceOfBirth, EducationCredential

3) Disposition

Definition. Latent capacities or susceptibilities that manifest under specified triggers or conditions.

4) FunctionProfile

Definition. Designed or learned operating patternâ€”what the thing or unit is for (objective + performance envelope).

5) SocialDesignation

Definition. Roles, offices, responsibilities, and memberships borne within social/legal regimes; they are labels of 
participation, realized in Activities.

6) Marker

Definition. Identifiers bound to entities for resolution/trace; not addresses and not lineage facts.

7) RelationalAttribute

Definition. Asserted edges between entities. These are standing ties, recorded on the subject with a pointer (relates_to) 
and optional scope/dates. Use them for personâ†”person ties and other static links. (Personâ†”organization affiliations are 
SocialDesignation > Membership.)


7a) Dependency (under RelationalAttribute)

Definition. Functional reliance: the subject needs or is provisioned by the target to perform functions or maintain states.
Core forms. depends-on / requires / provided-by / supplies-to / supported-by / supports.

7b) CausalInfluence (under RelationalAttribute)

Definition. Standing claim that the subject modulates the targetâ€™s outcomes/risks (outside any single narrated event).

Definition: 
actuality is an artificial term to describe any kind of predicate other than a copula.

actuality object reflects the following concepts:

â€¢ operative_verb â€” normalized predicate in dot-path form.
â€¢ participants list â€” the references to all entity objects that describe all entities participating in actuality.

â€¢ Examples:

actuality JSON Object:

applied to the verbatim sentence to surface additional semantics. The result should populate the "normalized" 
field.
